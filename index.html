Compost Crazy

New Yorkers aren't necessarily known for following the rules. But data from the city's sanitation department show residents are slowly getting on board with the mandated compost program.

By: Laura Pellicer — Dec. 3, 2025

From pizza crusts to coffee grinds, New Yorkers are developing the habit of separating compostable materials from trash. Now, one year since the program launched across all boroughs, residents continue to "smash composting records."

In one week alone, New Yorkers diverted more than 6 million pounds of food and yard waste.

City data show that in 2024 the amount of compost collected in the five boroughs was up more than 50% compared with 2013 - the first year the city started collecting compost from residences and institutions.

And not that it's a competition, but according to the Department of Sanitation's press office, Staten Island residents have produced the most compostable material per capita since the program started.

Despite the city's compost participation rates, an analysis by a sustainability professor at Baruch College shows that in 2024, residents still trashed 95% of compostable materials.
